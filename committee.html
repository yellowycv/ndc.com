Welcome to NDC Committee

The natural law of Attraction of Homogenous Species compels everything living to come together to work together. Individuals come together to form the family unit. Family units come together to form landlord associations. Landlord associations form communities, villages, towns, cities, etc. The communities are to have a governing body. This governing body will consist of committees representing different sectors of human endeavors.

Core Activity Committees:

  • Law & Law Enforcement
  • Health & Wellness
  • Environment
  • Needs Assessment
  • Treasury
  • Project Management
  • Family Services
  • Educational Development
  • Security
  • Life Essentials
  • Public Work

These committees will be employed beginning at the staffs of NDC. It is expected that other committees will evolve as the need arises. Part of the paradigm shift that must happen if we want to avoid the mistakes of the past is the recognition of the spirituality of man. All the committees should define their mode of operation. Tasked with identifying and planning on key areas, to bring change to our communities through new beat approaches in restoration of all society facets. With guidance of the steering committee that is responsible for safeguarding rights and coordinates the development of procedures in the other committees. The Committees focus on investigating and developing orders for rights restoration, and evaluate Restoration projects that objectively fit with needs assessment findings.

The Tentative Committee

  • 1. Security: The Security Committee is responsible for overseeing all systems, policies, and procedures related to security. This committee aims to form strategic partnerships with external groups to secure public water, food supplies, and air quality. The committee works with the governing committee to ensure that safety policies and laws are properly implemented.
  • 2. Treasury: The Treasury & Budget also referred to as the Finance Committee is responsible for managing and maintaining the financial status of Nasarawa Diamond Council. It is mandated in approving funds and funding for projects in all committees. To prevent corruption, this committee promotes and monitors transparency in all areas and subjects itself to external audits.
  • 3. Environment: This committee is designed to work with local and global communities to ensure the full restoration, maintenance, and monitoring of the health of the earth, including all-natural life on it. The Committee for Nature Conservation and Landscape Management is responsible for the protection and preservation of nature, natural habitats, and wildlife in Nasarawa state.
  • 4. Education: The Education Promotion Committee is intended to cover all areas of education including, but not limited to, true human history, spirituality, science & technology, and personal empowerment. The educational programs should take into account and promote the individual's different learning styles. The Education Promotion Committee is responsible for developing, promoting, and overseeing educational programs that meet the needs of children and adults.
  • 5. Family Service: The Committee for the Welfare of the Family is accountable for developing and supervising programs that serve the needs of families in Nasarawa State. With cognizance that every family is exceptional, thus all programs ought to be beneficial enough to serve the general public but still have the flexibility to address the uniqueness of each family. Provide comprehensive services to families by working with the appropriate committees within the NDC including, but not limited to, health and wellbeing, education promotion, and needs assessment.
  • 6. Health & Wellness: The Committee for Health and Wellbeing is responsible for promoting individual self-responsibility and providing all resources to ensure health and wellbeing for body, mind, and soul. Each community should provide facilities and design programs that are readily available and aim to meet the unique needs of each person.
  • 7. Communication: This committee aims to keep abreast of the vogue advances in all technologies with the aim of using them to serve the people of Nasarawa State more efficiently through their respective community assemblies. The Technology Committee is responsible for providing and overseeing the technical framework, guidelines, and procedures underlying the services offered by the Nasarawa Diamond Council.
  • 8. Project Management: The project planning committee is responsible for implementing and monitoring standards and procedures for planning and managing projects in all committees. These standards should use the highest level of technology and be designed in such a way that they evolve and adapt as new technologies and techniques become available in the areas of project management. The committee for project planning is to guide and supervise all projects financed by the assembly.
  • 9. Public Work: The committee is mandated to promote accessibility, affordable and safe movement of people, goods, and services through the construction and maintenance of roads infrastructure. Public works that are a combination of physical assets, management practices, policies, and personnel necessary to provide and sustain structures and services essential to the welfare and acceptable quality of life for all citizens.
  • 10. Need Assessment: The Needs Assessment Committee is accountable for assessing the needs of the people and the communities in which they live. This committee is expected to develop programs, guidelines, and community partnerships to accurately identify current or future problems and to assess what is needed to best solve those problems. This committee works together with the corresponding committees within NDC in order to plan, design, implement, manage, and finance required projects. Consequently, this committee also oversees the progress and acts as a feedback platform for the community that stimulated the need and the project that was created to meet that need.

How to Join a Committee

Become a member of the Organization by frequently attending weekly meetings to learn and understand the vision and mission of NDC, CARE, and the restoration of our planet Earth as a whole.
